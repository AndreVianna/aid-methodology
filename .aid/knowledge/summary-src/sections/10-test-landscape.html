Test Landscape

18 unit/integration test suites under tests/canonical/, glob-discovered and run by the tests/run-all.sh aggregator, all sharing the tests/lib/assert.sh assertion library. Most are pure Bash; two shell out to node (the .mjs validators) and two to pwsh (the PowerShell mirrors), each skipping if its runtime is absent. They cover the canonical helper scripts that AID skills depend on, plus discovery doc-ownership and the declared doc-set resolve/dispatch logic. CI runs the suites on PR/push (`.github/workflows/test.yml`), pins node + asserts pwsh present, and is a required status check on master. (per test-landscape.md)

Coverage gaps

The .mjs validators, the PowerShell mirrors, and the setup.* install flow are now covered (CI asserts node + pwsh present so those suites can never silently skip). The two new agent-format emitters carry their own generator self-tests run by the CI generator-selftests job (test_copilot_emitter.py — Copilot real-YAML round-trip; test_antigravity_emitter.py — Antigravity rule reshape), alongside test_manifest_safety.py — these live under .claude/skills/aid-generate/scripts/, not tests/canonical/. The genuinely untested surface is the prompt-driven orchestration layer: the /aid-* skills (verified by dogfooding; the aid-reviewer sub-agent is the closest adversarial integration check), the renderer (covered by its own VERIFY (deterministic) byte-identity gate across the 5 profiles, not tests/canonical/), and the end-to-end pipeline. There is no statement/branch coverage measurement. (per test-landscape.md §Coverage gaps and roadmap)

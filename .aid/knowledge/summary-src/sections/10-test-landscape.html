Test Landscape

49 unit/integration test suites under tests/canonical/ (plus the native-Windows tests/windows/Test-AidInstaller.ps1), glob-discovered and run by the tests/run-all.sh aggregator, all sharing the tests/lib/assert.sh assertion library. Most are pure Bash; some shell out to another runtime and skip when it is absent (by command -v guard: 7 to pwsh, 12 to python3, 6 to node). They cover the canonical helper scripts that AID skills depend on, the installer/CLI/release surface, the dashboard reader/server and the 1.0.0→1.1.0 upgrade migration, plus discovery doc-ownership and the declared doc-set resolve/dispatch logic. CI runs the suites on PR/push (.github/workflows/test.yml + installer-tests.yml cross-platform), and is a required status check on master. (per test-landscape.md)

Coverage gaps

The .mjs validators, the PowerShell mirrors, and the installer/CLI/release surface are now covered (CI asserts node + pwsh present so those suites can never silently skip; the native-Windows path runs on the installer-tests.yml Windows runner). The two new agent-format emitters carry their own generator self-tests run by the CI generator-selftests job (test_copilot_emitter.py — Copilot real-YAML round-trip; test_antigravity_emitter.py — Antigravity rule reshape), alongside test_manifest_safety.py — these live under .claude/skills/generate-profile/scripts/, not tests/canonical/. The genuinely untested surface is the prompt-driven orchestration layer: the /aid-* skills (verified by dogfooding; the aid-reviewer sub-agent is the closest adversarial integration check), the renderer (covered by its own VERIFY (deterministic) byte-identity gate across the 5 profiles, not tests/canonical/), and the end-to-end pipeline. There is no statement/branch coverage measurement. (per test-landscape.md §Coverage gaps and roadmap)

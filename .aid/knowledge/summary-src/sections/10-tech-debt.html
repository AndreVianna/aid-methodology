C1 — Mermaid CDN fetch not version-pinned or SHA-verified
Type: Supply Chain · Effort: S · Priority: P1

fetch-mermaid.sh fetches mermaid@latest from npm registry with no version pin and no SHA verification at download time. SHA is computed after download (not before). An npm-registry compromise would silently ship compromised JS into the offline KB viewer. Fix: pin PINNED_VERSION + EXPECTED_SHA256 constant and verify after download. (per tech-debt.md C1)

H2 — No CI
Type: Process · Effort: M · Priority: P2

Zero pre-merge automation. Every test/verify pass is manual. No .github/, no .gitlab-ci.yml, no Jenkinsfile. Quality gates are the AID skills themselves, exercised by dogfooding. (per tech-debt.md H2)

H3 — No language lock files
Type: Supply Chain · Effort: M · Priority: P2

No package-lock.json, no requirements.txt — vulnerability scanning is impossible. This is a sibling to C1. (per tech-debt.md H3)

H5 — Methodology flexibility
Type: Architecture · Effort: L · Priority: P2

Methodology assumes a rigid 16-doc KB set. Meta-repos, docs-only projects, and library-only projects need flexibility to adapt the standard set. (per tech-debt.md H5)
